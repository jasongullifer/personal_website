I am a PhD Candidate in Cognitive Psychology and Language Science at The Pennsylvania State University. Broadly, I am interested in bilingual language processing and psycholinguistics. I first realized my interest in languages in my German classes in high school. During my undergraduate studies at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst, I became interested in how people process language, and I decided to pursue Linguistics and Psychology as my major. After receiving my B.A. in 2007, I joined the Center for Language Science as a Research Technologist. Working for the Center, I helped conduct psycholinguistic studies on bilingual language processing before entering the graduate program in Psychology. In 2011 I received my M.S. from Penn State, and I am on track to defend my dissertation in December of 2014.

You can find out more about my interests by visiting my Research page.
